# BringsAbout

## Description

BringsAbout is a relation from Event to Situation. It links an event to the situation resulting from the event's occurrence: the brought-about situation obtains at the event's end point and represents the effects of that event at the moment it ends.

The supplied formal accounts characterize the brought-about situation as unique for each event. One source presents this as a unique maximal situation brought about by an event, while another formalization states that each Event has exactly one brought-about Situation.

BringsAbout is used in event-causation accounts together with Triggers. In these accounts, Triggers relates a situation to an event it activates, while BringsAbout relates an event to the resulting situation. When an event brings about a situation that is then related through Triggers to another event, the first event can be treated as directly causing the second under the source-specific causation account.

One source also uses BringsAbout in the characterization of HistoricalDependence, where an event, or one of its parts, may bring about a situation that is related through Triggers to another event, or may bring about a situation necessary for that triggering.
