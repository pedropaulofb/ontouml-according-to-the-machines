# Mediation

## Description

Mediation is the relation used to connect a Relator to the endurants or substantial universals it mediates. At the instance level, Mediation holds between a Relator individual and a mediated endurant when the Relator includes a qua individual that inheres in that endurant. The same source family treats this connection as an existential-dependence link: a Relator depends on the mediated participants that it connects.

At the universal or modeling level, Mediation is characterized as a formal relation between a Relator universal and the endurant or substantial universals whose instances participate in the relational context. In conceptual models, it connects an explicitly represented Relator to the classes representing mediated entities. This relation is distinct from a Material relation: the Material relation is derived from, or grounded in, the mediating Relator rather than being represented only as an unconstrained tuple.

Several sources present Mediation as central to Role-based classification. A Role is associated with a Relator through Mediation, and some source-specific contributions state that Role and RoleMixin classifiers must be connected to a Mediation relation. In the event-extension source, this current relationship-based pattern is contrasted with HistoricalRole, which is associated through Participation to an Event rather than through Mediation to a Relator.

The source-specific contributions also support two constraints at a descriptive level: every Relator mediates at least two distinct individuals or endurants; and one Chapter 6 contribution further states that the bearer of a Relator is the mereological sum of the individuals mediated by it. Some sources additionally characterize Mediation as a binary dependency relation whose source end is a Relator and whose mediated target end is constrained by cardinality and read-only requirements; these details are left for the Stereotype Profile section.

In multi-level and powertype-oriented settings, Mediation is also used to relate role-like classifications to relator classifications. One source indicates that specialized Mediation links can align a taxonomy of relators with a taxonomy of roles, allowing role types to be categorized according to the type of Relator their instances bear.
