# ComponentOf

## Description

ComponentOf is a parthood relation used for functional-complex contexts. The supplied sources characterize it as relating a functional part or component to a functional complex, and also as a parthood relation between functional complexes when mandatory parthood is interpreted as direct functional parthood. In these accounts, ComponentOf combines proper parthood with individual functional dependence under the relevant functional types.

A functional complex is treated as a whole whose components contribute to its functionality or behavior. This distinguishes ComponentOf from collective-membership parthood: components of a functional complex may play multiple role types in the internal organization of the whole, rather than a uniform member role type. The supplied sources also classify ComponentOf as a material relation and connect it to generic dependence between a whole and replaceable functional parts.

The intermediate contributions consistently do not support treating ComponentOf as unrestrictedly transitive. They present functional parthood as non-transitive in general and assign ComponentOf the meta-property of non-transitivity, while also recording narrower cases in which transitivity is supported. In particular, transitivity is supported across chains of essential component parthood, and Chapter 7 reports transitivity for specific direct functional parthood readings while stating that transitivity among ComponentOf relations holds only in certain contexts. Those contexts may require domain knowledge and can be delimited using patterns grounded in relators, qua individuals, and existential dependency relations.

The supplied Chapter 8 contribution identifies ComponentOf as a commonly used conceptual parthood relation and reports standard UML aggregation/composition notation as the proposed representation for shareable and non-shareable variants. It also records non-reflexivity, anti-symmetry, non-transitivity, and weak supplementation as meta-properties, and constrains both association ends to universals whose instances are functional complexes.
