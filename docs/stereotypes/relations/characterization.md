# Characterization

## Description

Characterization connects a characterized universal or type with a characterizing moment universal or type. Across the Phase 1 sources, the common commitment is that instances of the characterized endurant or substantial universal bear instances of the characterizing moment, Mode, or Quality universal. At the instance level, this is grounded in inherence: instances of the characterizing moment, Mode, or Quality inhere in instances of the characterized universal or type, with some sources emphasizing that the bearer is unique.

The supplied files present the direction of Characterization differently across contexts. Some formulate the relation from the characterized universal or type to a moment type, while others describe it from an intrinsic moment universal to the substantial universal it characterizes or as a profile relation whose source end is a Mode. This Phase 1 consolidation therefore records the safe common reading as the association between a characterized type and a characterizing intrinsic moment type, without fixing a final relation-end profile.

In modeling terms, Characterization is used when qualities, modes, or other reified descriptive features are represented explicitly and tied to the universals whose instances bear them. The relation supports the elementary specification of universals by features and the grounding of classifications of substantials by intrinsic moments. One source identifies the intrinsic moments involved as Quality and Mode universals, not Relator universals.

One source-specific contribution further relates Characterization to Role and attribution: a Role universal can be represented as characterized by a qua individual universal, and attribution understood as non-sortal classification is said to be founded on Characterization. Another source presents a multi-level use in which Characterization can align a taxonomy of intrinsic moments with a taxonomy of substantials, so that specialization criteria for a Quality or Mode universal can correspond to specialization criteria for the characterized substantial universal.
