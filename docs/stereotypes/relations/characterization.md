# Characterization

## Description

Characterization relates a characterized universal or type to a moment universal or type whose instances inhere in instances of the characterized universal or type. Across the Phase 1 sources, this relation is used to connect endurant or substantial universals with intrinsic moment universals, especially Quality and Mode universals, so that instances of the characterized universal bear instances of the characterizing moment. The intermediate sources describe the relation with different orientations in prose: some present the characterized endurant type as characterized by a moment type, while others identify the relation from an intrinsic moment universal to a substantial universal. The shared grounding is the instance-level inherence relation between an individual moment, Quality, Mode, or Trope and its bearer.

Characterization supports the specification of universals by explicitly represented features. In this role, a feature may be modeled as a moment universal, and Characterization records the connection between the specified universal and the feature borne by its instances. Several source-specific contributions also ground Characterization in the modeling of reified descriptive properties: when a descriptive property is represented as an individual Quality or comparable moment-like entity, instances of that reified property inhere in instances of the bearer class.

The intermediate material gives two source-specific extensions of this central reading. First, in the context of Role and qua individual universals, Characterization is presented as an alternative way to account for role-specific dependent moments, and non-sortal attribution is said to be founded on Characterization to a qua individual universal. Second, in multi-level modeling patterns, Characterization may be specialized to align a taxonomy of intrinsic moments with a taxonomy of substantials, including second-order Quality or Mode types and second-order substantial types.

Profile-level details remain candidates for later validation. One source-specific profile treatment describes Characterization as a formal, binary, existential-dependence, and functional relation, with a Mode at the source end, a characterized universal at the target end, target cardinality exactly one, a read-only target end, and source minimum cardinality at least one. Another source used in Phase 1 does not use the target relation label in the cited passages; it only provides provisional support through inherence and bearing constraints for Tropes, Qualities, Dispositions, and concrete individuals.
