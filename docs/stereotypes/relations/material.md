# Material

## Description

Material characterizes relations that have their own material or ontological ground rather than holding directly between relata. Across the Phase 1 sources, Material is contrasted with formal relations: formal relations hold directly, while material relations depend on an additional grounding entity, such as a relator or, in some accounts, an externally dependent mode. The sources also describe these grounding entities as truthmakers for material relations, especially where the relation cannot be reduced to the relata themselves or to their intrinsic properties.

Several sources present Material as a relation derived from a Relator. In this account, the Relator mediates the relata, and the Material relation is the relation that holds between those relata in virtue of that Relator. The Material association is therefore distinguished from the Relator that founds or explains it: relator individuals instantiate relational properties, while the derived Material relation represents the fact or condition obtained through the mediated entities.

The Phase 1 sources also connect Material to OntoUML modeling patterns. One source-specific profile account states that every `material` association is derived, must be connected to exactly one derivation relation founded on a Relator, and receives association-end cardinality constraints from the corresponding Mediation relations, with minimum cardinalities of at least one. Another source frames the modeling pattern as a compound representation: a Relator universal, Mediation relations from that Relator to the mediated classes, the derived Material association, and a derivation relation connecting the Relator to the Material relation. These constraints should be treated as Phase 1 source-grounded material for later profile validation.

The sources further relate Material to role and qua-individual analyses. In that setting, role classification, qua individuals, relators, Mediation, and Material relations are connected through the relational conditions that roles depend on. One source characterizes a relational qua individual as an externally dependent mode that a substantial has in the context of a material relationship, while another states that a relator universal can induce a material relation between anti-rigid functional restrictions.

One later source revises the earlier classification by treating many extrinsic relations formerly labeled material as descriptive relations that deserve reification because they hold in virtue of individual qualities of the relata. For this Phase 1 consolidation, that contribution is best read conservatively as a truthmaking-oriented qualification of the earlier relator-centered account, not as a resolved replacement for the OntoUML Material stereotype.
