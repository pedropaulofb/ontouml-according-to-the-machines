# Comparative

## Description

Comparative is treated in the Phase 1 inputs as a formal relation of comparison. It is distinguished from Material because it does not require an intervening Relator; its grounding is instead in intrinsic properties or qualities of the related entities.

The inputs characterize Comparative as a derived formal or descriptive relation. Its immediate relata can be qualities, and the relation may be reduced to relations between qualia obtained through attribute functions. The meta-properties of Comparative are derived from relations among qualia in the relevant quality structure, connecting the relation to the geometry, ordering, or topology of quality domains.

As a formal relation, Comparative can be represented through tuples with predefined arity and through multiple-tuple cardinality constraints in standard association representation. This contrasts with Material relations, which the inputs associate with Relator universals and mediated entities.

One Phase 1 source also indicates that Comparative should not be categorically excluded from reification. When the qualitative relationship itself is useful as a domain entity, the relevant individual qualities of the relata may form a quality complex or reified Comparative relationship. This allows information about the relationship itself to be represented without reducing the relationship only to a tuple, while preserving the relationship's identity across changes in its internal configuration.
