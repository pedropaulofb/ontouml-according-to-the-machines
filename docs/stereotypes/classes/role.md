# Role

## Description

Role is an anti-rigid sortal stereotype for contingent classifications whose application depends on relational or otherwise external conditions. An individual may enter or leave a Role without losing its identity, because the identity principle is supplied by an associated Kind, ultimate sortal, or otherwise taxonomy-supported identity-providing type rather than by the Role itself. Across the supplied files, Role is consistently characterized as relationally dependent: it classifies instances in virtue of relations or relational contexts, not merely in virtue of intrinsic conditions.

Role is distinguished from Phase because both are anti-rigid, but Role classification is relationally dependent whereas Phase classification is treated as relationally independent or intrinsic. Role is also distinguished from RoleMixin: a Role classifies instances that share a common ontological nature or identity principle, while a RoleMixin can cover entities governed by different identity principles.

Several source-specific contributions connect Role to current relationships, Relator, and Mediation. In those contributions, a Role is grounded in a relational context and may be associated through Mediation with a Relator. This supports a Phase 1 distinction between ordinary Role classifications grounded in current relationships and HistoricalRole or processual-role-like classifications grounded in past event participation. The supplied files support this distinction only as a first consolidated characterization, not as a completed stereotype profile.

The intermediate files also preserve a scope distinction across source generations. Earlier sources formulate Role mainly as a specialization of a substance sortal or Kind, while later OntoUML 2-oriented sources generalize Role to endurant types beyond substantials, including Relator, Mode, and Quality types when the taxonomy supports externally dependent anti-rigid classification. In part-whole contexts, the supplied files support using Role to represent contingent positions or participation in a whole; this should not be conflated with essential or inseparable parthood.

At the profile level, the supplied files report that Role should not subsume rigid classifiers and that its relational restriction condition should be represented explicitly, with later files emphasizing connection to Mediation. These constraints remain Phase 1 source-grounded consolidation points and are not final expert-validated profile rules.
