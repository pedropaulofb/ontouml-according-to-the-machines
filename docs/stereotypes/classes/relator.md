# Relator

## Description

Relator represents relator universals or relator types whose instances are relators. Across the supplied sources, relators are characterized as relational moments: existentially dependent endurants that depend on a plurality of individuals and connect or mediate at least two distinct entities. The sources consistently distinguish this relational endurant from a tuple, fact, or event-like entity.

A Relator is repeatedly analyzed as an aggregate composed of qua individuals that share a foundation. In this account, qua individuals stand in parthood relations with the Relator, and the Relator mediates the corresponding participants. This analysis connects Relator to Mediation and Material relations: Relator universals can induce Material relations between mediated universals, and explicit Relator classes can be connected to the mediated entity classes through Mediation relations.

For conceptual modeling, the supplied sources favor representing Relator explicitly when the model needs the ontological foundation of a Material relation or relational context. This separates the relational foundation from the derived relation itself and helps avoid ambiguity that can arise when a Material relation is represented without the supporting Relator. Relators also ground Role-related modeling: Role instantiation and relational specialization can be analyzed through the Relator-mediated context in which the participating entities stand.

Several sources characterize Relator as a reified or truthmaking relationship entity. On this view, the existence of the Relator at a time supports the corresponding relation holding at that time. Because a Relator is an endurant, it may preserve its identity while its contingent properties or internal qualitative configuration change. This supports the distinction between mutable ongoing relational phenomena modeled as Relators and events or processes understood as their manifestations or historical unfoldings.

At the type-classification level, the supplied sources treat Relator as a category for classes whose instances are relators. One OntoUML 2 source characterizes Relator as an ultimate-sortal stereotype for classes whose instances are relators. A multi-level modeling source further states that Relator Universals are disjoint from Quality Universals and Mode Universals, cannot specialize substantial universals, and may be classified using either intrinsic properties of relators or properties of the connected relata.
