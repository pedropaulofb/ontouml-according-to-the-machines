# PhaseMixin

## Description

PhaseMixin is an anti-rigid, relationally independent non-sortal used to capture contingent phase-like classifications that are not confined to a single sortal identity line. It defines contingent properties for its instances and aggregates properties common to different phases. Several source-specific intermediates also note that the notion emerged after the original set of UFO endurant types and was later incorporated into OntoUML 2-style modeling support.

As a non-sortal, PhaseMixin does not supply a uniform principle of identity. Its instances may be ultimately governed by different Kind classifiers, may follow different identity principles, and are not constrained to a single ontological nature. The consulted intermediates characterize PhaseMixin classification as crossing ultimate-sortal boundaries; sources focused on substantials describe it as classifying substantials of different kinds through common intrinsic properties, while one intermediate reports that a revised theory generalizes phase mixins from substantial types to endurant types more broadly.

PhaseMixin is distinguished from Phase because it applies across different Kind classifiers rather than within one sortal identity line. It is also distinguished from RoleMixin by the kind of contingent condition involved: the intermediates characterize PhaseMixin in terms of intrinsic contingent properties or conditions, while RoleMixin is treated as the other anti-rigid non-sortal.

The intermediate files report several constraints relevant to PhaseMixin. Non-sortals, including PhaseMixin, may not specialize sortals or sortal universals, and rigid or semi-rigid classifiers may not specialize anti-rigid classifiers such as PhaseMixin. One source also presents second-order PhaseMixin patterns as partitioning a mixin universal according to mutable intrinsic properties, and states that a second-order type specializing PhaseMixin may have Category, PhaseMixin, or RoleMixin as its base type.
