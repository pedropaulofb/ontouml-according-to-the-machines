# Quantity

## Description

Quantity is used for representing amounts or portions of matter as identifiable individuals in conceptual models. The supplied sources distinguish undifferentiated amounts of matter from model individuals with determinate identity and countability. On that basis, Quantity functions as the modeling counterpart for otherwise non-countable matter when it must be represented as an individual with identity-sensitive semantics.

Across the intermediate files, Quantity is characterized as an objectified or maximally self-connected portion of matter. One source makes this characterization explicitly topological and reserves Quantity for substantials rather than quality values or measurements. Another source cautions that Quantity should not be treated as homeomerous in a problematic infinite-divisibility sense, while still allowing quantities to be composed of other quantities.

The supplied sources treat Quantity as a kind whose instances are quantities. In the OntoUML profile material represented here, Quantity is introduced as a concrete specialization of Substance Sortal and as one distinction among kinds based on the ontological category of their instances. One UFO source also places quantities within the partition of substantials, disjoint from objects and collectives.

Parthood is central to the consolidated characterization of Quantity. The intermediate files repeatedly describe quantities as extensional individuals: their identity is determined by the sum of their parts, all of their parts are essential, and removing a part yields a different quantity. This supports the treatment of parthood involving quantities as essential parthood and connects Quantity to the constraints associated with SubQuantityOf relations. One source further situates quantities as generically dependent on the continuants they constitute, rather than as specifically dependent on a single container or bearer.
