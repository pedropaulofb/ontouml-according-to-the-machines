# Quality

## Description

Quality is an OntoUML 2 ultimate-sortal stereotype for classes whose instances are qualities. In the supplied intermediate files, qualities are consistently characterized as intrinsic moments or particularized properties that existentially depend on bearers. They are distinguished by their relation to quality structures: a quality can be mapped or projected into a quality structure, and the corresponding point or value in that structure is a quale.

A Quality instance should therefore be distinguished from both its bearer and its value. Several source-specific contributions emphasize that qualities are numerically distinct individuals, may maintain identity while their qualitative value changes, and can be represented independently from the regions or values used to measure or classify them. The supplied sources also distinguish simple qualities from complex qualities, where complex qualities bear further qualities associated with dimensions of the relevant quality domain.

Quality structures provide the value spaces used to account for measurement, qualitative change, attribute values, and Datatype domains. In the consolidated evidence, quality universals or quality types are associated with quality structures, quales are members or points of those structures, and a quality is connected to a quale through a value relation. Some sources use this account to interpret attributes as functions derived from quality universals rather than as direct representations of the quality individuals themselves.

Several intermediate files extend the account from intrinsic qualities to relational qualities and reified descriptive properties. In these accounts, relational qualities inhere in one relatum while depending on another relatum or relational context, and relationships or Relator instances may be constituted by sums or complexes of individual qualities. These contributions support treating qualities as reification units for descriptive properties and descriptive relations, while still distinguishing a quality from any determinate property value it has at a time.

Event-oriented sources add that Event instances can bear or manifest qualities, and that events may bring about the creation or destruction of intrinsic and relational qualities. These claims are source-specific and should not be read as a complete Phase 1 account of the Event stereotype. A multi-level source further treats Quality Universal as disjoint from Mode Universal and Relator Universal, and supports second-order quality classifications based on regions of an associated quality structure.
