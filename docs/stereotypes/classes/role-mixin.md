# RoleMixin

## Description

RoleMixin represents an anti-rigid non-sortal whose instantiation is contingent and relationally or externally dependent. It abstracts common role-like properties across multiple role types or base kinds, rather than supplying a principle of identity for its instances.

As a non-sortal, RoleMixin does not provide a uniform identity principle. Its instances may follow different identity principles, and later sources characterize them as not constrained to one specific ontological nature. This distinguishes RoleMixin from Role: Role is treated as a sortal role for individuals of a single kind, whereas RoleMixin supports contingent role-like classification across heterogeneous kinds or identity lines.

Several source-specific contributions describe RoleMixin as a dispersive or mixin universal that aggregates properties common to different roles. Earlier accounts emphasize common relational specialization conditions across roles carried by different kinds; later accounts extend the same pattern to endurant types more generally, including cases where the classified instances may be substantials, relators, modes, or qualities when the model supports that interpretation.

The supplied profile-oriented contributions consistently treat RoleMixin as abstract and as constrained by its non-sortal and anti-rigid character. Reported constraints include that RoleMixin must not specialize sortals, that rigid or semi-rigid classifiers must not specialize anti-rigid classifiers such as RoleMixin, and that RoleMixin is associated with relational dependence conditions. Some sources formulate this dependence through mediation or through a restriction condition with a minimum cardinality of at least one.

One multi-level source further uses RoleMixin in second-order patterns, where role-like classifications apply to entities of different kinds through common relational properties. This use remains grounded in RoleMixin's core characterization as an anti-rigid mixin classification based on a relational context.
