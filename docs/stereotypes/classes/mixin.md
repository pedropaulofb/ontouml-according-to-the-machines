# Mixin

## Description

Mixin is a non-sortal stereotype for classifying properties shared by entities that may belong to different kinds. Across the supplied intermediate files, Mixin is associated with dispersive or non-sortal universals: it does not supply a uniform principle of identity, may classify entities that follow different identity principles, and can be used to abstract properties common to instances of otherwise distinct sortals.

Several supplied sources characterize Mixin more specifically as a semi-rigid non-sortal. Under this characterization, Mixin represents properties that are essential to some instances and accidental to others, so the classification is not uniformly rigid or uniformly anti-rigid. One supplied source describes the unqualified mixin stereotype as non-rigid and contrasts it with Category as rigid and RoleMixin as anti-rigid; the consolidated Phase 1 characterization therefore preserves the safer shared point that Mixin is a non-sortal whose applicability may vary across instances.

The supplied profile-oriented contributions report that a Mixin class cannot have direct instances and must be abstract. They also associate Mixin with constraints against specializing sortals, since a non-sortal or mixin universal would otherwise inherit a principle of identity. Additional supplied constraints state that Mixin cannot specialize anti-rigid classifiers such as RoleMixin, and that second-order specializations of Mixin Universal should use a mixin universal, rather than a sortal or moment universal, as their base type.

One supplied contribution also connects mixins with characterizing universals and attribution: characterizing universals may be represented as mixins to improve model structure, although the same source notes that they are often represented through attributes or attribute values depending on their determinable or determinate status. This supports treating Mixin as a construct for predicative characterization rather than as an identity-supplying sortal.
