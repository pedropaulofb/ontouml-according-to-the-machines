# Situation

## Description

Situation characterizes a particular configuration of part of reality, or a state of the world, understood as a whole at a specific time point. The intermediate sources align this notion with states of affairs and emphasize temporal binding: qualitatively indistinguishable configurations at different time points are numerically distinct situations. In the event-oriented account used by the sources, situations are the pre- and post-event correlates through which change is described: Event occurrences map the world from situations to situations.

Situation is also used to connect endurants, facts, time points, dispositions, and Events. Endurants can be characterized by properties, including relational properties, within situations. Some sources describe situations as activating dispositions whose manifestations are Events. One formalization treats Situation as a ConcreteIndividual, disjoint with Endurant and Event, and as the superclass of Fact.

Factual situations are facts: a Fact is characterized as a Situation that obtains at a time point. The valid intermediate sources distinguish these obtaining situations from counterfactual situations while preserving Situation as the broader category.

Situation participates centrally in event triggering, event outcomes, and event causation. A Situation may trigger an Event when the Event occurs because that Situation obtains at the Event's begin point, and an Event may bring about a Situation when the Event results in that Situation obtaining at its end point. The sources support a causal pattern in which an Event directly causes another Event by bringing about a Situation that triggers the latter Event. Some formal accounts assert uniqueness constraints for triggering and brought-about situations, including a unique triggering Situation for an Event occurrence, a unique maximal Situation brought about by an Event, or exactly one triggering Situation and exactly one brought-about Situation for each Event.
