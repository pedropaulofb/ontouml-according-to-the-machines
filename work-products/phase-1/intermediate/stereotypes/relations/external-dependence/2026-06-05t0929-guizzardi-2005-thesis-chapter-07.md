# External Dependence

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 uses external and existential dependence to explain role-related entities, especially qua individuals. It harmonizes role accounts by interpreting role-instance-like entities as qua individuals: externally dependent modes that inhere in their bearers and depend on another entity through the context captured by a relator (section 7.3).

The source states that qua individuals composing a relator are existentially dependent on each other. In the functional-parthood analysis, existential dependence among functional qua individuals underpins the treatment of generic functional dependence, individual functional dependence, and the transitivity arguments for functional parthood (sections 7.3 and 7.4).

This source-specific contribution does not define a standalone External Dependence profile. It supports only the dependence commitments explicitly used in Chapter 7 for roles, qua individuals, relators, and functional parthood.
