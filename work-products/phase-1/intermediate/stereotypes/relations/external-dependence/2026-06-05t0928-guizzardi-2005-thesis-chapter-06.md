# External Dependence

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 defines external dependence through the notion of externally dependent modes. A mode is externally dependent when it is existentially dependent on an individual that is independent of its bearer. Source locator: p. 238.
- The source uses external dependence to capture cases in which an individual has a mode by virtue of association with an external entity. The independence condition excludes dependence on the bearer itself, on the bearer's other moments, and on essential parts or wholes of the bearer. Source locators: pp. 238-239.
- External dependence is part of the source's account of qua individuals and relators. Qua individuals are treated as complex externally dependent modes, and relators aggregate qua individuals that share dependencies and foundation. Source locators: pp. 239-240.
- For conceptual modeling, the source's supported contribution is that dependencies of moments on external endurants should be represented explicitly when they ground relators or materially founded relations. Source locators: pp. 239-241; pp. 260-262.
