# Historical Dependence

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- The source distinguishes historical descriptive relations from merely historical non-descriptive relations within its relation taxonomy (p. 5).
- Historical descriptive relations are said to hold in virtue of a past quality of a relatum, while merely historical relations hold in virtue of an event that occurred in the past (p. 5).
- For non-descriptive historical relations, the source says the relation itself need not be reified as a relationship, but explicitly modeling the past event may still have practical advantages (p. 12).
- This contribution supports treating historical dependence as grounded in past qualities or past events, while keeping relationship reification limited to descriptive cases.
