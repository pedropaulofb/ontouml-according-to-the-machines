# Historical Dependence

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source defines `<<historicalDependence>>` as the association stereotype for historical dependence between events. It captures dependencies grounded in causal or precondition-like relations between events and the situations they bring about or trigger.

The source gives four conditions for historical dependence: an event or one of its parts brings about the situation that triggers another event or one of its parts; it brings about a situation necessary but not sufficient for such triggering; it brings about a situation necessary and more than sufficient for such triggering; or the dependence holds transitively through another historically dependent event.

The source states that these conditions include direct causation and, with transitivity, indirect causation. It also explicitly distinguishes historical dependence from mere temporal precedence: historical dependence is a stronger relation that can represent direct and indirect causation as well as dependence without causation.
