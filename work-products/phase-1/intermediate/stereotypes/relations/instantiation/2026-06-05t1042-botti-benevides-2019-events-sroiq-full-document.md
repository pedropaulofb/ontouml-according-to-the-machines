# Instantiation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source reifies universals and individuals in the domain of quantification and uses `instantiates` to relate individuals to universals (p. 4-5, Section 3.1; p. 5, Figure 1). It deliberately uses an atemporal instantiation relation for the SROIQ-oriented formalization, noting that temporalized instantiation would introduce additional representation trade-offs (p. 5, Section 3.1).

The source states that individuals instantiate at least one universal and approximates the principle of instantiation for universals through a cardinality constraint on `instantiates` (p. 5, Section 3.1; p. 17-18, axioms a97, a111). It constrains the possible instances of specific universal categories: `QualityUniversal` can only be instantiated by qualities, `ObjectUniversal` by objects, `EventUniversal` by events, and `ParticipationUniversal` by participations (p. 5-6, U1-U4; p. 18, a121-a124).

For event modeling, the source states that events instantiate event universals rigidly across their occurrence or existence (p. 5, Section 3.1). It also treats `plays` as the relevant specialization of `instantiates` from objects to processual roles in the FOL theory, while noting limitations in expressing the corresponding constraint in SROIQ (p. 5-6, U5; p. 18, Section 4.2.2).
