# Instantiation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 introduces instantiation as a basic formal relation between individuals and universals. In the source's notation, x::U means that individual x is an instance of universal U, or that x has the property of being a U. Source locator: p. 218.
- The source constrains instantiation so that its first argument is an individual and its second argument is a universal. This distinguishes instantiation from set membership and from inherence. Source locators: pp. 203-204; p. 218.
- The extension of a universal is defined as the set containing exactly its instances. However, the source also emphasizes that universals are not identical to their extensions and must be understood intensionally. Source locator: p. 219.
- The source differentiates instantiation from exemplification. Instantiation relates an individual directly to a universal, whereas exemplification of a moment universal occurs through a particular moment inhering in an individual. Source locators: pp. 219-220.
