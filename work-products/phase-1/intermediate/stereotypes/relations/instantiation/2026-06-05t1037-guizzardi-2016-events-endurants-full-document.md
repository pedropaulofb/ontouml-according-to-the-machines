# Instantiation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source uses instantiation as the relation between individuals and types. It distinguishes necessary instantiation, where an individual instantiates a kind in all possible situations in which it exists, from contingent instantiation, where an individual may enter or leave a type's extension without ceasing to be the same individual.

This distinction is central to the source's characterization of kinds, phases, roles, and role mixins. Kinds provide identity and must be instantiated necessarily; phases, roles, and role mixins are contingent classifications of endurants.
