# Component Of

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 contributes to Component Of through its treatment of mandatory parthood relations between functional complexes. It interprets mandatory parthood between such complexes as direct functional parthood, defined at the instance level by parthood plus individual functional dependence (section 7.4).

The source distinguishes two direct functional parthood readings, depending on the direction of individual functional dependence. It proves that both direct functional parthood readings are transitive, but it does not conclude that all mandatory functional parthood chains are transitive (section 7.4).

The final result is that mandatory parthood between complex objects, i.e., functional parthood, is non-transitive in general: transitivity is guaranteed only for certain structural patterns and blocked in others. Chapter 7 connects this analysis to the componentOf relation, stating that transitivity among componentOf relations only holds in certain contexts and that those contexts can be delimited using patterns grounded in relators, qua individuals, and existential dependency relations (sections 7.4 and 7.5).
