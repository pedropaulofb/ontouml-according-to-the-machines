# Component-Of

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source supports `Component-Of` through its account of component-functional-complex parthood. A functional complex is a whole whose components contribute to the functionality or behavior of the whole. Unlike collectives, whose members can be treated as playing a uniform role type, functional complexes can contain components that play multiple role types in the internal organization of the whole.

The source presents component-functional-complex parthood as central for conceptual modeling but does not treat it as unconditionally transitive. It states that isolating contexts for complexes can require domain knowledge, because the relevant unifying relations may be complex. However, the source does support transitivity across chains of essential component parthood: for complexes, essential components entail functional dependence, and chains of essential parthood preserve the relevant dependence and parthood commitments.
