# Comparative

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- The source treats comparative relations as intrinsic descriptive relations when they are derivable from intrinsic qualities of their relata (pp. 4-5, 8).
- It rejects the assumption that comparative relations never deserve reification: comparative relations may be reified when the qualitative relationship itself is a useful domain entity (p. 4).
- The minimal weak truthmaker of a comparative relation is the mereological sum of the relevant individual qualities of the relata; that sum forms a quality complex or reified comparative relationship whose internal configuration can change while the relationship remains identifiable (p. 8).
- Reification permits additional information about the comparative relationship itself without reducing the relationship to a tuple (pp. 3, 8).
