# Comparative

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 classifies comparative relations as formal relations of comparison. Unlike material relations, they do not require an intervening relator. Source locator: p. 236.
- The immediate relata of comparative formal relations are qualities. Such relations can be reduced to relations between qualia obtained through attribute functions. Source locators: p. 236; p. 254.
- The meta-properties of a comparative formal relation are derived from the meta-properties of relations among qualia in the relevant quality structure. This connects comparative relations to the geometry, ordering, or topology of quality domains. Source locator: p. 255.
- Because comparative relations are formal relations represented by tuples with predefined arity, the source treats their cardinality constraints as unambiguously multiple-tuple constraints and allows standard association representation. Source locator: p. 258.
