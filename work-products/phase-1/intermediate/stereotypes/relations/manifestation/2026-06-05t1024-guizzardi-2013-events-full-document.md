# Manifestation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source treats `manifestation` as the connection between dispositions and events. Dispositions are particularized properties that inhere in objects, may fail to be manifested, and, when manifested, are manifested through events.

For the events considered in the paper, atomic events are manifestations of dispositions. The formal account states that each atomic event is associated with a unique disposition manifested by that event.

The manifestation relation also explains the existential dependence of atomic events on objects. If a disposition is manifested by an atomic event and that disposition inheres in an object, then the atomic event depends on that object.

In the source's summary, atomic events are manifestations of single dispositions, while complex events may be manifestations of several dispositions. This connects the event account to the ontology of endurants by relating event occurrence to object-inherent dispositions.
