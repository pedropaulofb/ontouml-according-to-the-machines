# Manifestation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source uses manifestation to connect events with the qualities or relationships that are expressed through those events. In the paper's terminology, when an event is a manifestation of qualities, those qualities are the focus of the event.

For relational episodes, the source states that the episode manifests the relationship. The way the relata participate in the episode depends on the qualities that constitute the relationship.

Manifestation therefore differs from mediation and material derivation. Mediation connects a relator to its relata, and a material relation is derived from that relator; manifestation connects an event-like episode to the qualities or relationship whose occurrence it expresses.

This supports a source-specific account in which a relationship can be an endurant truthmaker of a relation while an event can be its manifestation, rather than the relationship itself.
