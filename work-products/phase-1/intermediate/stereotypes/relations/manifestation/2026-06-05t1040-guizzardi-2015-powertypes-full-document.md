# Manifestation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source adopts the notion of manifestation from the theory of variable embodiments. A variable embodiment is associated with a principle that selects a rigid embodiment at a world or time; that selected rigid embodiment is its manifestation.

This relation is central to the source's preferred account of powertype instances. A powertype instance is not identified with a fixed abstract universal or with a mereological sum. Instead, it can have different manifestations while preserving identity through the relevant principle of variable embodiment.

The source also uses manifestation to explain property attribution. Some properties of the variable embodiment hold in virtue of properties of its manifestation, especially resultant properties derived from the current manifestation. Other properties, including direct and regularity properties, are attributed to the variable embodiment itself. The source further allows that a variable embodiment may fail to be manifested in a given world or time while still being referable.
