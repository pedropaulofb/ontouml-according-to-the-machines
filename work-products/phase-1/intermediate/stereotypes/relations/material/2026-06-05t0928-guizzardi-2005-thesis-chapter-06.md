# Material

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 distinguishes material relations from formal relations. Material relations have material structure of their own and are mediated by relators, whereas formal relations hold directly without an intervening individual. Source locators: pp. 236-237.
- A relation is material when there is a relator universal such that the relation's condition is obtained through instances of that relator universal mediating the relata. The source treats the material relation as derived from the relator universal. Source locators: pp. 241-242.
- For conceptual modeling, the source recommends a compound representation: a class for the relator universal, mediation relations to the mediated entity classes, a material association for the derived material relation, and a derivation relation between relator and material relation. Source locators: pp. 260-262.
- The explicit material-relation pattern addresses two issues discussed by the source: overloaded association-class semantics and ambiguity between single-tuple and multiple-tuple cardinality constraints. Source locators: pp. 256-260.
