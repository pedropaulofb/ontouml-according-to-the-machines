# Material

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source treats material relations as extrinsic relations: qualitative relations whose truthmakers are genuinely relational entities and cannot be reduced to the relata themselves or to the relata's intrinsic properties. This contrasts material relations with formal relations and with intrinsic qualitative relations.

A material relation holds because there is an additional entity that grounds it. This truthmaker depends on the relata and may also involve further dependencies, but it is not identical to the mathematical tuple representing the relation extension.

The source argues that material relations are especially important for conceptual modeling because many modeled domain relations have this extrinsic character. Such relations require explicit treatment of their truthmakers when the model must capture constraints, temporal development, or modal properties of the relationship.

In the OntoUML pattern discussed by the source, a material relation is derived from a relator through mediation links. The relation is what holds between the relata; the relator is the relationship-like endurant that explains why and how it holds.
