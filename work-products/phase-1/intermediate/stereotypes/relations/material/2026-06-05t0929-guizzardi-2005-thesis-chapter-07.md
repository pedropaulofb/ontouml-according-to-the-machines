# Material

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 treats Material relations as derived from relator universals in the analysis of roles. The role's relational specialization condition is further analyzed as a material relation derived from a relator universal (section 7.3).

The source also relates material relationships to qua individuals. It states that a relational qua individual is an externally dependent mode that a substantial has in the context of a material relationship. This supports the Chapter 7 account in which role classification, qua individuals, relators, mediation, and material relations are connected (section 7.3).

In the functional-parthood analysis, a relator universal is said to induce the material relation between anti-rigid functional restrictions. This contribution is limited to the relator-derived treatment of Material relations in Chapter 7.
