# Material

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.2.2 and table 8-2, `material` represents a material relation, i.e., a relational universal induced by a relator universal. The source contrasts material relations with the relators that found them: relator individuals are the actual instantiations of relational properties, while material relations represent facts derived from relators and their mediated entities.

In the proposed profile, every `material` association must be connected to exactly one derivation relation whose foundation is a `relator`. The cardinality constraints of the material relation's association ends are derived from the corresponding `mediation` relations of the founding relator, and each end has a minimum cardinality of at least one. The source also prescribes that every `material` association has `isDerived = true`.
