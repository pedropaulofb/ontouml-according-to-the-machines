# Mediation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source characterizes mediation as the relation that connects a relator to the endurants it mediates. Mediation is presented as a special form of existential dependence: a relator exists only insofar as the relevant mediated relata exist.

Mediation is central to the derivation of a material relation from a relator type. For a derived relation to hold between relata, the source requires an instance of the relator type that mediates those relata. The material relation is therefore grounded in the mediating relator rather than represented merely as an unconstrained tuple.

The source uses mediation to address cardinality ambiguity in extrinsic relations. By explicitly representing the relator and its mediation links, a model can state constraints over the participants of each relationship instance rather than only over the tuples of a derived association.

Within the paper's analysis, mediation belongs to the formal apparatus used to connect truthmakers and relata. It should not be conflated with the material relation that is derived from the relator.
