# Mediation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 defines an instance-level mediation relation between a relator and an endurant. A relator mediates an endurant when a qua individual that is part of the relator inheres in that endurant. Source locators: pp. 240-241.
- The source requires every relator to mediate at least two distinct individuals. It also stipulates that the bearer of a relator is the mereological sum of the individuals mediated by that relator. Source locator: p. 240.
- At the universal level, mediation holds between a universal and a relator universal when every instance of the universal is mediated by an instance of the relator universal. Source locator: p. 241.
- In conceptual modeling, mediation connects an explicitly represented relator universal to the classes representing mediated entities. The source classifies these links as formal relations between universals, which blocks an infinite regress of material relations. Source locators: p. 258; p. 262.
