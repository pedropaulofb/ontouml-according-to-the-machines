# Mediation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.2.2 and table 8-2, `mediation` is a formal relation between a relator universal and the endurant universal or universals it mediates. At the instance level, the source maps this relation to existential dependence between relator individuals and mediated individuals.

The source constrains `mediation` as a binary dependency relation. A `mediation` relation must have a `relator` at its source association end; the mediated target end must have minimum cardinality at least one and be read-only; the relator source end must also have minimum cardinality at least one; and every `mediation` association is binary. The source also uses mediation to refine relationally dependent classifiers: every `role` and every `roleMixin` must be connected to a `mediation` relation.
