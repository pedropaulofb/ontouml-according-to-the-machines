# Mediation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 gives Mediation a central role in the relation between Role and Relator. It states that every role universal bears a mediation relation to a relator universal, and it gives this as a consequence of analyzing a role's relational specialization condition as a relator-derived material relation (section 7.3).

The source also provides a profile-level implication: a class stereotyped as Role must be connected to an association end of a Mediation relation. This is the only explicit profile-level constraint supported by Chapter 7 for this source-specific contribution (section 7.3).

In the functional-parthood analysis, mediation is also used to define when a relator connects two individuals. Chapter 7 introduces a predicate that holds exactly when there is a relator mediating the two individuals (section 7.4).
