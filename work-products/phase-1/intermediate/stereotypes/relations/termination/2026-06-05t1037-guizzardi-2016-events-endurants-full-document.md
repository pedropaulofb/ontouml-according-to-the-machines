# Termination

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source supports termination as the counterpart of creation in the life cycle of an endurant. Events may bring about situations in which endurants go out of existence; the proposed pattern also treats endurants as having active and inactive phases and a final life after the relevant manifestations have accumulated.

In the source's modeling pattern, finalization and termination dates specialize relations such as created in and terminated in. Once an endurant is causally inactive, its properties can no longer be manifested and its qualities are immutable with respect to their values. This makes termination relevant to distinguishing active endurant life from completed historical manifestation.
