# Characterization

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.2.2 and table 8-2, `characterization` is a formal relation between a mode universal and the endurant universal that the mode universal characterizes. At the instance level, the source maps this relation to inherence between an individual mode and its bearer.

The source treats characterization as an existential-dependence relation, a binary formal relation, and a functional relation. In the proposed profile, a `characterization` relation must have a `mode` at its source end; its target end, connected to the characterized universal, must have cardinality exactly one and must be read-only; its source end must have minimum cardinality at least one; and the relation must be binary.
