# Characterization

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source identifies characterization as the relation from an intrinsic moment universal to a substantial universal. It applies to quality universals and mode universals, not relator universals, and makes explicit that the intrinsic moments in question inhere in instances of the characterized substantial universal (Section 2.1, p. 3; Section 5.3.1, p. 15).

In the second-order patterns, characterization can be specialized to align a taxonomy of intrinsic moments with a taxonomy of substantials. A second-order substantial type can have instances characterized by corresponding instances of a second-order quality or mode type, with a one-to-one correspondence used in the source to express that one classification is defined in terms of the other (Sections 5.3.1-5.3.2, pp. 15-16).

For Phase 1, this source supports documenting characterization as the link used to ground classifications of substantials by qualities or modes, especially when second-order types categorize intrinsic moment universals.
