# Characterization

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source uses `inheresIn` and its inverse bearing reading; it does not use the target relation label in the cited passages. This contribution is therefore provisional and should be reviewed before consolidation.

In the source theory, `Trope` is an `Endurant`, and both `Disposition` and `Quality` are subtypes of `Trope` (p. 5, Figure 1; p. 15, axioms a16-a18). `inheresIn` relates tropes to concrete individuals, with each trope inhering in exactly one concrete individual (p. 7-8, D1', D5; p. 16-17, axioms a51, a77, a101). Objects and events are required to bear at least one trope through the inverse of `inheresIn` (p. 8, Section 3.3, D5; p. 17, axiom a117).

The source imposes structural constraints on `inheresIn`: because the domain `Trope` is not disjoint from the codomain `ConcreteIndividual`, the theory explicitly asserts asymmetry and intransitivity for `inheresIn` (p. 8, Section 3.3, D6-D7). For dispositions, the bearer of a disposition is present in all situations in which the disposition is present (p. 8, Section 3.3, D9).
