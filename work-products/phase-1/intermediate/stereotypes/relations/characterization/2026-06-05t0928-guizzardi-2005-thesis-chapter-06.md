# Characterization

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 defines characterization as a relation between a universal and a moment universal: a universal is characterized by a moment universal when every instance of the former exemplifies the latter. Source locator: p. 221.
- The relation supports the elementary specification of universals. Features in such a specification may be moment universals, and characterization captures the connection between the specified universal and those features. Source locators: pp. 220-221.
- At the instance level, characterization is grounded in inherence: instances of the characterizing moment universal inhere in instances of the characterized universal. Source locators: pp. 220-221; p. 249.
- For conceptual modeling, the source uses characterization to relate explicitly represented quality or mode universals to the universals whose instances bear them. Source locators: pp. 248-253.
