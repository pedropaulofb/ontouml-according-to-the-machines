# Characterization

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 treats Characterization as an alternative formulation of the dependence between Role and qua individual universal. After stating the mediation axiom for Role and Relator, the source states that a role universal can alternatively be represented as characterized by a qua individual universal (section 7.3).

The source further generalizes this point by saying that attribution, understood as non-sortal classification, is founded on a characterization relation to a qua individual universal. For the Role contribution in Chapter 7, Characterization links the role universal to the qua individual universal that accounts for the role-specific dependent moments (section 7.3).

This source-specific contribution does not provide a complete independent profile for Characterization; it grounds the relation through the Role, qua individual, and attribution discussion in Chapter 7.
