# Member Of

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source analyzes `memberOf` in the context of interpreting powertype instances as collectives or populations. In that interpretation, for each subtype of a base type, there may be a collective whose members are exactly the instances of that subtype.

The source notes that the member-of relation shares relevant formal properties with instantiation, including irreflexivity, asymmetry, and anti-transitivity. This formal resemblance helps explain why a collective interpretation may appear attractive for powertype instances.

The source ultimately rejects membership-based collectives as the identity of powertype instances. Membership change would change the extensional sum, and lack of current members would undermine existence. In the preferred variable-embodiment view, membership is retained at the level of rigid embodiments or populations that manifest the variable embodiment, rather than defining the stable identity of the powertype instance.
