# Member Of

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.3.2 and table 8-4, `memberOf` is a parthood relation between a singular entity, as part, and a collective, as whole. The part may be either a functional complex or a collective considered as a unity; the whole must be a collective.

The source assigns `memberOf` the meta-properties non-reflexivity, anti-symmetry, intransitivity, and weak supplementation. Although transitivity does not hold across two `memberOf` relations, the source states that transitivity does hold across `memberOf` followed by `subCollectionOf`. A `memberOf` relation can represent essential parthood only when the whole is extensional; in that case, all parthood relations in which that whole participates are essential parthood relations.
