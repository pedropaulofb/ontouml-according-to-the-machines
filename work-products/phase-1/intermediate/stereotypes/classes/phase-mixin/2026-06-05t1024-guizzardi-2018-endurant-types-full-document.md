# Phase Mixin

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `phase mixin` as an anti-rigid, relationally independent non-sortal that aggregates properties common to different phases (Section 2, p. 5). It also notes that the notion of phase mixin was not part of the original set of UFO endurant types but emerged as useful in later modeling practice (Section 2, p. 5). The proposed theory generalizes phase mixins from substantial types to endurant types in general (Introduction, pp. 2-3; Section 4, pp. 11-12).

In the formal theory and profile, «phaseMixin» is an anti-rigid non-sortal, distinct from «roleMixin» (Section 3, pp. 7-8; Section 4, p. 11). As a non-sortal, it does not provide or carry a single principle of identity, and the profile does not explicitly capture the ontological nature of instances of non-sortal classes (Section 4, p. 12). The supported constraints are that non-sortals cannot specialize sortals, and that rigid or semi-rigid classifiers cannot specialize anti-rigid classifiers such as «phaseMixin» (Table 1, p. 13).
