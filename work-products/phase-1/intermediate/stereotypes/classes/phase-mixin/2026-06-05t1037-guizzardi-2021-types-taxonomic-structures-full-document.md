# Phase Mixin

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to the supplied source, `«phaseMixin»` is the OntoUML 2 stereotype for independent anti-rigid non-sortals (Section 4.1, p. 10). The paper identifies phase mixin as a useful notion that emerged after the original set of UFO endurant types and defines it as a dispersive type aggregating properties common to different phases (Section 2, p. 4). As a non-sortal, a phase mixin does not provide a uniform identity principle; its instances may follow different identity principles and are not constrained to a specific ontological nature. In the revised theory, phase mixins capture contingent intrinsic classifications across ultimate-sortal boundaries (Section 4.1, p. 10).
