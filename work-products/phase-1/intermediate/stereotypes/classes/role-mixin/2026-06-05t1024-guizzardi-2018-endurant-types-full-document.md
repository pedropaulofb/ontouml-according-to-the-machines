# Role Mixin

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `role mixin` as an anti-rigid, relationally dependent non-sortal that aggregates properties common to different roles (Section 2, p. 5). The proposed theory generalizes role mixins from substantial types to endurant types in general, so role-mixin classification may apply over endurant types whose instances are substantials, relators, modes, or qualities when supported by the model (Introduction, pp. 2-3; Section 4, pp. 11-12).

In the proposed profile, «roleMixin» is one of the anti-rigid non-sortal stereotypes, distinct from «phaseMixin» (Section 3, pp. 7-8; Section 4, p. 11). As a non-sortal, it does not provide or carry a single principle of identity, and the profile does not explicitly capture the ontological nature of instances of non-sortal classes (Section 4, p. 12). The supported constraints are that non-sortals cannot specialize sortals, and that rigid or semi-rigid classifiers cannot specialize anti-rigid classifiers such as «roleMixin» (Table 1, p. 13).
