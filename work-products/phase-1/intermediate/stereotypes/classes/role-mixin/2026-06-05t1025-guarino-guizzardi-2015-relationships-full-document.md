# Role Mixin

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source presents role mixin as an OntoUML pattern that can be combined with the relator pattern once relationships are treated as endurants. The pattern is described as useful for handling a recurrent modeling problem concerning roles.

A role mixin supports a contingent classification that may be shared across different kinds of individuals. In the source's application to relationships, this means that different relator kinds can fall under a common contingent role-like supertype when they stand in the relevant relational context.

The role mixin pattern is important in the paper because it shows that the modal taxonomy used for object types can also organize relationship types. This follows from the claim that relators have identity, modal properties, and change profiles comparable to other endurants.

For this source-specific contribution, role mixin is grounded as a pattern-level construct for grouping roles across multiple base kinds, including relator kinds.
