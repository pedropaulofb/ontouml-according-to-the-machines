# Role Mixin

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 uses Role Mixin for a role-like non-sortal that covers different role types. It is introduced for cases in which a role-relevant classifier has instances belonging to different kinds and therefore cannot supply one principle of identity for all its instances (section 7.1).

In the design pattern of section 7.1, the Role Mixin captures the common relational specialization condition shared by multiple sortal role subclasses. Those role subclasses may have direct instances and carry the principles of identity supplied by appropriate kinds. The Role Mixin itself is abstract in the pattern and is relationally dependent on another type.

The source also extends the parthood treatment of anti-rigid universals to role mixins. Where specific dependence from instances of roles or role mixins to their parts is only warranted under de dicto modality, Chapter 7 uses immutable part rather than essential part (section 7.2).
