# Subkind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

A `subkind` is treated in the source as a rigid subtype of a `kind`. It inherits the principle of identity supplied by the `kind` it specializes, rather than introducing a separate ultimate principle of identity.

The proposed profile requires every `subkind` to have a unique `kind` as a supertype. It also constrains `subkind` so that its supertype is not a `phase`, `role`, or `roleMixin`, preserving the prohibition against rigid sortals specializing anti-rigid classifiers.

The source distinguishes `subkind` specialization from phase classification. A `subkind` generalization set may form a partition, but it is not required to do so. The source also notes that the `subkind` stereotype can be omitted in conceptual specifications without loss of clarity.
