# Subkind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source's contribution to subkind is limited to its role in the powertype-pattern configuration shown and discussed in the running model. In that configuration, subkinds appear as explicit specializations of a base kind within a generalization set associated with a powertype.

The accompanying analysis explains that the instances of the powertype correspond to explicit subtypes of the base type. The subkind contribution is therefore to identify the lower-level subtype position in the pattern: a subkind classifies ordinary base-level instances, while its associated reified counterpart is treated as an instance of the powertype.

The source does not provide a standalone modal definition of subkind. Its substantive contribution for this stereotype is the placement of subkind as a base-level specialization used to connect ordinary classification with multi-level powertype classification.
