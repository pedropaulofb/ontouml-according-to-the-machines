# Subkind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

In this source, a subkind is a rigid sortal specialization of a kind. The source groups kinds and subkinds together as the rigid sortals in the endurant-type taxonomy, while distinguishing subkinds from kinds.

A subkind is therefore not introduced as an independent provider of identity. Its instances remain governed by the unique kind they necessarily instantiate, and the subkind refines that kind without changing the underlying identity principle.
