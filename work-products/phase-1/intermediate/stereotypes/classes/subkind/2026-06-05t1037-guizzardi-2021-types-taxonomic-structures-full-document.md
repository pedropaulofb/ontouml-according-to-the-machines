# Subkind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to the supplied source, `«subkind»` is the OntoUML 2 stereotype for rigid sortals whose instances share an ontological nature and follow a unique identity principle (Section 4.1, p. 9). The source explains subkinds as rigid sortals that carry, rather than provide, the principle of identity supplied by a unique kind (Section 2, p. 4). In OntoUML 2, a subkind must specialize a unique ultimate sortal, from which it inherits both the identity principle and the restriction to a single ontological nature. Unlike traditional OntoUML, this is not restricted to substantial types: subkinds can refine endurant kinds of different ontological natures when the taxonomy supports that refinement (Section 4.1, p. 9).
