# Subkind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.1.2 and table 8-1, `subkind` is a rigid, relationally independent restriction of a substance sortal. It carries the principle of identity supplied by the corresponding substance sortal rather than introducing a new ultimate principle of identity.

The source treats `subkind` as a rigid sortal specialization and constrains it accordingly. Following the cited postulate 4.3, a `subkind` cannot have an anti-rigid universal as a supertype; in the profile presented in Chapter 8, this excludes `phase`, `role`, and `roleMixin` from the general collection of a `subkind`.
