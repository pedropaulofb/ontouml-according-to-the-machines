# Subkind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source characterizes `subkind` as a rigid sortal that does not supply a new principle of identity, but instead carries the principle of identity supplied by a unique kind (Section 2, p. 4; Section 4, p. 11). In the proposed profile, «subkind» is not restricted to substantial types: it may decorate endurant sortals of any supported ontological nature, relying on the ultimate kind it specializes to determine that nature (Section 4, p. 11).

The supported constraints are that every non-kind endurant sortal, including «subkind», specializes an ultimate kind stereotype from the proposed profile, and that a class cannot specialize more than one kind (Table 1, p. 13). As a rigid type, «subkind» is also covered by the constraint that rigid types cannot specialize anti-rigid types (Table 1, p. 13).
