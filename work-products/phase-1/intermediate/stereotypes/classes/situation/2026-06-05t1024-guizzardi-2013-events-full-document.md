# Situation

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source characterizes `Situation` as a particular configuration of part of reality that can be understood as a whole. It aligns the notion with state of affairs while distinguishing situations by binding them to specific time points; qualitatively indistinguishable configurations at different time points are numerically distinct situations.

The source distinguishes factual and counterfactual situations. Factual situations are facts, and facts are situations that obtain at particular time points. In the formal account, a fact is a situation that eventually obtains in some time point.

Situations are central to the source's account of event-based change. A situation may trigger an event when the event occurs because that situation obtains; a situation may also be brought about by an event when the event occurrence results in that situation obtaining at the event's end point. The paper postulates a unique triggering situation for each event occurrence and a unique maximal situation brought about by each event.

The source uses situations to define a restricted form of event causation: if an event brings about a situation and that situation triggers another event, the first event directly causes the second.
