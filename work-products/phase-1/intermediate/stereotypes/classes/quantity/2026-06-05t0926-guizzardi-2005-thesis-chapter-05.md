# Quantity

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source grounds `Quantity` in the problem of representing amounts of matter in conceptual models. It argues that undifferentiated mass terms do not by themselves provide individuation or counting criteria, and therefore require nominalization before their instances can be treated as model individuals.

The supported modeling interpretation is that a quantity is an objectified portion of matter: a maximally self-connected piece of some matter kind, constituted by portions of that matter and suitable for identity-sensitive conceptual modeling. The source explicitly proposes `«quantity»` for the kind whose instances are quantities in this sense.

Under this interpretation, a quantity is not treated as homeomerous in the problematic infinite-divisibility sense. It may be composed of other quantities, but all of its parts are essential to it: removing a part yields a different quantity. The source also situates quantities as generically dependent on the continuants they constitute, rather than as specifically dependent on a single container or bearer.
