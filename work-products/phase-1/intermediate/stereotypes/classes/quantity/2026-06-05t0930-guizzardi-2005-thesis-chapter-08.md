# Quantity

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.1.2 and table 8-1, `quantity` is introduced as a concrete specialization of Substance Sortal. The source characterizes a quantity as a substance sortal whose instances are quantities and treats quantity kinds as one of the three distinctions made within the category of kinds on the basis of the ontological category of their instances.

The source also connects `quantity` to the treatment of meronymic relations in section 8.3.2. Entities stereotyped as `quantity` are treated as extensional individuals: all their parts are essential, and parthood relations involving quantities are consequently essential parthood relations. The `quantity` stereotype is therefore relevant both to classification by substance sortal and to the constraints governing `subQuantityOf` relations.
