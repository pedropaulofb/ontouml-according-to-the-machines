# Quantity

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 distinguishes amounts of matter from objects. Amounts of matter are substantials without unity; their parts are essential, and their identity is determined by the sum of their parts. Source locator: p. 215.
- The source states that conceptual models require instantiated objects to have determinate identities. Therefore, to represent amounts of matter in conceptual models, the source introduces quantities as maximally self-connected amounts of matter. Source locator: p. 216.
- A quantity is treated as a genuine object for modeling purposes because it has both determinate identity and determinate countability. This supports quantity as the modeling counterpart used when otherwise non-countable matter must be represented as an identifiable individual. Source locator: p. 216.
