# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

A class stereotyped as `role` represents the phased-sortal category of roles. In the source, roles are anti-rigid sortals: an instance may move into or out of the role while preserving its identity under the relevant substance sortal.

The source distinguishes roles from phases by the kind of specialization condition involved. For a role, the condition depends on extrinsic relational properties. An entity plays a role in a context demarcated by relations with other entities.

For the proposed profile, the relational restriction condition is part of the definition of the role type. Consequently, if an association represents the restriction condition of a `role`, the minimum cardinality at that association end must be at least one. The profile also requires every `role` to have a unique `kind` as supertype and disallows anti-rigid roles from subsuming rigid kinds.
