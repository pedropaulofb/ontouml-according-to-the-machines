# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source recalls `<<role>>` as a contingent endurant type instantiated in a relational context. Instances of a kind can instantiate roles contingently in different relational contexts, and the source contrasts this current relationship-based role playing with historical role playing in past events.

For the source's contrast, `<<role>>` is associated through `<<mediation>>` with a `<<relator>>`, while `<<historicalRole>>` is associated through `<<participation>>` with an `<<event>>`. This supports a distinction between roles grounded in current relationships and historical roles grounded in past event participation.

Under the source's historical semantics, a current relationship role may cease when the relevant current relationship no longer holds, whereas a historical role remains grounded in the accumulated past event. This contribution is restricted to the source's contrast between ordinary roles and historical roles.
