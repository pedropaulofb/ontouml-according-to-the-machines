# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source characterizes roles as contingent, relationally dependent sortal types of endurants. An individual may enter or leave a role without losing identity, but role membership depends on relational properties rather than only intrinsic ones.

Because roles are sortals, the source requires their instances to belong to the same kind. This distinguishes roles from role mixins, which are also contingent and relationally dependent but classify entities of multiple kinds. The source also connects social roles to relational endurants: social relators can induce social roles whose life cycles and changes are constituted by associated events.
