# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source provides a part-whole-specific contribution to `Role`. It supports using roles to represent context-dependent positions that objects occupy in the internal organization of wholes. In this setting, role modeling makes explicit that an object can participate in a whole under certain circumstances without that participation being part of its identity-supplying nature.

The source uses role types to clarify the structure of collections and complexes. In collectives, member parts are characterized as playing a uniform role type relative to the whole. In functional complexes, by contrast, different components may play multiple role types in the organization of the complex.

The source also qualifies the role-based view of structure. Because roles are anti-rigid universals, modeling the internal structure of a whole only in terms of objects playing roles implies contingent participation. The source therefore distinguishes such contingent participation from essential or inseparable parthood.
