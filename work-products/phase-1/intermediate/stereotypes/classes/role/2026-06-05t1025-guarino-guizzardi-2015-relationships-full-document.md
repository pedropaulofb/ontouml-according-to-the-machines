# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source characterizes role as a contingent type: an individual may instantiate a role during part of its existence and later cease to instantiate it without losing identity. This places role with phase among the modal categories that apply contingently rather than necessarily.

The paper applies this modal account to relationships by treating relators as endurants. A relator can therefore play a role when it participates in an appropriate relational context, while remaining the same relator when that role no longer applies.

This use of role depends on the paper's main ontological move: relationships must be modeled as endurants if they are to bear modal properties and undergo genuine change. Under that view, role classifications can be used for relator types as well as object types.

The source supports role as a taxonomic and modal modeling device for contingent classifications, including contingent classifications of relationship-like endurants.
