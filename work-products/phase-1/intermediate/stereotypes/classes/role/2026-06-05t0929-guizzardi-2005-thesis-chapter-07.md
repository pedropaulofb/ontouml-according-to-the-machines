# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 treats Role as an anti-rigid, relationally dependent universal. A Role specializes a substance sortal according to a relational specialization condition, so the role classification is contingent for its instances while their identity is supplied through an appropriate substance sortal rather than by the role itself (sections 7.1 and 7.3).

The source distinguishes this substantial-universal reading from accounts that treat roles as rigid classifiers whose instances are role objects or qua individuals. Chapter 7 reserves the Role name for the substantial universal corresponding to ordinary role classifiers, while the dependent role-instance-like entity is handled as a qua individual universal (section 7.3).

Chapter 7 refines the relational dependence of Role by analyzing the specialization condition as a material relation derived from a relator universal. Consequently, a Role bears a mediation relation to a relator universal and can alternatively be characterized by a qua individual universal (section 7.3).

For profile-level modeling, the source states that a class stereotyped as Role must be connected to an association end of a mediation relation. This Phase 1 contribution does not validate constraints outside Chapter 7.

For parthood, Chapter 7 states that anti-rigid universals such as roles cannot have essential parts in the strict de re sense. When a part is required while an individual instantiates a role, the source treats the commitment in de dicto terms and uses immutable part rather than essential part (section 7.2).
