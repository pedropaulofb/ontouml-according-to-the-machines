# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to the supplied source, `«role»` is the OntoUML 2 stereotype for externally dependent anti-rigid sortals whose instances share an ontological nature and follow a unique identity principle (Section 4.1, p. 9). The paper distinguishes roles from phases by their relational dependence: roles capture relational properties shared by instances of a kind (Section 2, p. 4). As an anti-rigid sortal, a role classifies instances contingently while preserving their identity through the ultimate sortal it specializes. In OntoUML 2, `«role»` is no longer restricted to substantial types and may classify endurants such as relators, modes, or qualities when those role-like conditions are supported by the taxonomy (Introduction; Section 4.1, pp. 2, 9).
