# Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source contributes only a contrastive characterization relevant to role modeling. It distinguishes `ProcessualRole` from relational roles and states that relational roles are inferred from the holding of a specific relationship (p. 10, Section 3.7). This distinction is presented as important for disambiguating different senses of roles in modeling and ontology engineering (p. 10, Section 3.7).

This source does not provide a full account of relational roles. Its contribution to this page should therefore be limited to the source-grounded contrast: relational roles are relationship-grounded, whereas processual roles are grounded in participation in events (p. 10-11, Section 3.7).
