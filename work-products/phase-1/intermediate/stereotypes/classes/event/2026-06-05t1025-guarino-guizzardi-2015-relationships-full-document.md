# Event

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source analyzes events as perdurants and contrasts them with endurants. Events can function as truthmakers in some cases, but the paper rejects them as the general ontological account of relationships because classical events do not genuinely change while maintaining numerical identity.

The paper introduces episodes as maximal events under a unity criterion. Relational episodes involve multiple participants standing in a relation and can entail that the relation holds while the episode lasts. However, this only partially captures the paper's target notion of relationship.

The source distinguishes event-like truthmakers from endurant relationships by their treatment of time and modality. An episode may have temporal parts with different properties, but it is not the same kind of bearer of ongoing qualitative change and counterfactual modal properties as a relator.

Events remain relevant in two ways. A past event may be an asynchronous truthmaker for a historical relation, and a relational episode may manifest a relationship. When a reified relation is only a fully determined historical record at an instant, the source allows an event-like interpretation; when qualitative or modal change matters, an endurant interpretation is required.
