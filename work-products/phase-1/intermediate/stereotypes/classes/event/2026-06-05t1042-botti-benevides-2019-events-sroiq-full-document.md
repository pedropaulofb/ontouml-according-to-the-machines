# Event

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source treats events as a central UFO-B category and frames UFO-B as the part of the Unified Foundational Ontology concerned with events and their relations to objects and situations (p. 2; p. 4, Section 3). In the presented formalization, `Event` is a `ConcreteIndividual`, is disjoint with `Endurant` and `Situation`, and is partitioned into `AtomicEvent` and `ComplexEvent` (p. 15-16, axioms a12, a25-a26, a34, a39-a40, a44).

The source characterizes atomic events through their manifestation relation to dispositions: each `AtomicEvent` is the manifestation of a unique `Disposition` (p. 6-7, Section 3.3, D2). It characterizes complex events mereologically: events may have other events as parts, `AtomicEvent` is defined by the absence of event parts, and `ComplexEvent` is the complement of `AtomicEvent` within `Event` (p. 6, Section 3.2, M1-M2). The event parthood relation is a strict partial order in the FOL theory and is constrained by weak and strong supplementation (p. 6, Section 3.2, M3-M9).

Events are temporally delimited by functional `beginPoint` and `endPoint` relations to `TimePoint`, with the begin point preceding the end point (p. 9, Section 3.5, T5'-T6'). Each event is triggered by a unique situation and brings about a unique situation, situating events as transitions between situations (p. 10, Section 3.6, S3-S4). The source also states that events can bear tropes, including qualities, and that event qualities cannot change because events are immutable (p. 8, D5; p. 11, Section 3.8).
