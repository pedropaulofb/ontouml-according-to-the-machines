# Event

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source presents `Event` as the UFO-B category for perduring individuals. Events happen in time by extending across temporal parts, in contrast with endurants, which are treated as wholly present whenever they are present.

For event mereology, the source distinguishes atomic and complex events. Atomic events have no proper parts, while complex events are aggregations of at least two disjoint events. The paper axiomatizes event parthood as a strict partial order and adds weak and strong supplementation, yielding an extensional event mereology in which complex events are individuated by their parts.

The source also treats events as ontologically dependent. Atomic events directly existentially depend on objects via `dependsOn`; complex events depend on their proper parts and, indirectly, on the objects on which those parts depend. This dependence supports the later notion of object participation in events.

Temporally, each event is associated with a begin point and an end point in a linear order of time points. The source uses these time boundaries to define Allen-style temporal relations between events and requires that the temporal extent of an event include the temporal extent of its proper parts.

Dynamically, events are characterized as transformations from one portion of reality to another. A triggering situation obtains at the event's begin point, and a situation brought about by the event obtains at its end point. The source further connects atomic events to dispositions by treating them as manifestations of dispositions inhering in objects; complex events may involve manifestations of several dispositions.
