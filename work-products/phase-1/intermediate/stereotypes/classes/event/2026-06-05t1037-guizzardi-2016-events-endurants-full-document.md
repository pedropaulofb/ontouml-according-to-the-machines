# Event

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source treats events as first-class ontological entities distinct from endurants. In this account, events capture change: they relate situations in which endurants bear intrinsic or relational properties and may create, destroy, or alter endurants and their qualities. Events may also involve endurants as participants playing processual roles.

Within the UFO account reported by the source, events have an extensional mereology, participate in temporal precedence relations, and enter causal structures in which situations brought about by events may trigger other events. The source further characterizes events as manifestations of particularized properties, especially qualities and dispositions, and therefore as existentially dependent on the endurants that bear those properties.

For structural conceptual modeling, the source emphasizes that events cannot be treated like mutable endurants. Under the classical event ontology adopted in the source, events cannot qualitatively change while preserving identity and cannot bear modal properties in the way endurants can. Consequently, object identifiers can safely designate events only once the relevant event is historical, i.e., once no further branching of its unfolding is possible. Ongoing phenomena that can change while maintaining identity should instead be represented through the underlying endurant whose manifestations accumulate as events.
