# Quality

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source treats qualities as individual dependent entities that can be bearers of change while maintaining identity. This is contrasted with classical tropes, which the source treats as unsuitable when genuine qualitative change must be represented.

Relational qualities are central to the source's account of relationships. A relational quality inheres in one relatum while depending on another relatum or on the relational context. Such qualities provide the fine-grained internal structure through which a relationship accounts for how a relation holds and develops.

A relator is analyzed as a variable embodiment constituted, at each time, by a mereological sum of qualities. The nuclear qualities make the relation hold; further dependent qualities form a shell that captures contingent aspects that may change, appear, or disappear during the relationship's life.

The source also connects qualities to events: events are treated as manifestations of qualities, and relational episodes are manifestations of the relationship constituted by those qualities.
