# Quality

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source classifies Quality as an intrinsic moment: a moment dependent on a single individual and measurable through a quality structure. Quality Universals have qualities as instances, and quality structures provide the value space used to account for measurement and change in quality values (Sections 2.1 and 5.3.1, pp. 2-3, 14-15).

For specialization constraints, Quality Universal is disjoint from Mode Universal and Relator Universal, and moment universals cannot specialize substantial universals. Consequently, second-order types specializing Quality Universal may only categorize instances of Quality Universal as base types (Section 5.3, p. 14; Section 5.4, p. 18).

The source supports second-order quality classifications based on regions of an associated quality structure. Depending on whether such regions overlap, the resulting categorization may be disjoint or non-disjoint. It also explains that taxonomies of qualities can be coordinated with taxonomies of substantials through characterization relations (Section 5.3.1, pp. 14-16).
