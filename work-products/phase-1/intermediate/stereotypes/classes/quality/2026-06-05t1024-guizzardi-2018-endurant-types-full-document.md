# Quality

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `quality` as a subtype of intrinsic moment that can be mapped to a quality space and may change its value while maintaining identity (Section 2, p. 4). The formal theory partitions intrinsic moments into modes and qualities, and defines quality types as endurant types whose instances are qualities (Section 3, p. 8).

For OntoUML profile interpretation, the source is transitional. It states that in the current version of OntoUML, «quality» carried information about the nature of instances of a moment type, because the language was silent about modal properties of moment types (Section 4, p. 10). In the proposed OntoUML 2.0 profile, «quality» is discontinued and replaced at the ultimate-sortal level by a distinct stereotype for quality kinds (Section 4, pp. 10-11). Therefore, this source contributes to a `quality` page by clarifying the ontological nature of qualities and by recording that the proposed profile separates instance nature from modal properties for quality types.
