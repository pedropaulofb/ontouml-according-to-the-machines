# Quality

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 defines qualities as moment individuals that instantiate quality universals. A quality universal is an intrinsic moment universal associated with a quality structure, and each quality has a quale in an associated quality structure. Source locators: pp. 223-224; pp. 233-234.
- Qualities are numerically distinct from their bearers and from one another, even when mapped to the same quale. This supports distinguishing an individual quality from the value or region by which it is represented. Source locators: pp. 214-215; p. 234.
- The source distinguishes simple qualities from complex qualities. Simple qualities do not bear other qualities, while complex qualities bear simple qualities corresponding to the dimensions of their associated quality domain. Source locators: pp. 232-233.
- Quality structures provide the foundation for attribute values and datatype domains in conceptual modeling. The same account also supports deriving meta-properties of comparative formal relations from the structures of the relevant quality domains. Source locators: pp. 245-251; pp. 254-255; p. 276.
