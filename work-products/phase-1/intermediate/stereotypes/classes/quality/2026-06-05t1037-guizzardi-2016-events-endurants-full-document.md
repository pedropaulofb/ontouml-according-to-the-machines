# Quality

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source treats qualities as existentially dependent endurants and as objectified properties of other endurants. A quality can undergo qualitative change while maintaining identity; this distinguishes the quality itself from the value it may have at a given time.

The source repeatedly groups qualities with dispositions as particularized properties of endurants. Events may manifest these particularized properties, and events may also bring about changes by creating or destroying intrinsic and relational qualities. In the source's endurant-event pattern, qualities and dispositions of active endurants can be manifested through events; when the endurant is causally inactive, its qualities are immutable with respect to their values.
