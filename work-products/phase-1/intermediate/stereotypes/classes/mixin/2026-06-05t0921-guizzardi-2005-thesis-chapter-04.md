# Mixin

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Conceptual modeling types classified as mixins represent dispersive universals. In the source, dispersive universals do not supply a principle of identity and cannot have direct instances. They can nevertheless structure a conceptual specification by abstracting properties shared across otherwise distinct classifiers.

The unqualified `mixin` stereotype represents non-rigid non-sortals. The source distinguishes this from `category`, which is rigid, and from `roleMixin`, which is anti-rigid. A non-rigid mixin represents a property that may be essential to some instances and accidental to others.

The proposed profile requires a `mixin` to be depicted as an abstract class. It also constrains its supertype so that it is not a `kind`, `subkind`, `phase`, `role`, or `roleMixin`, reflecting the postulate that a dispersive universal cannot specialize a sortal.
