# Mixin

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `mixin` as a semi-rigid non-sortal: it classifies properties shared by entities of different kinds, where those properties may be essential to some instances and accidental to others (Section 2, p. 5). In the formal theory, `mixin` is defined through the combination of semi-rigidity and non-sortality (Section 3, p. 7). The proposed profile gives «mixin» a direct UFO counterpart and reflects these properties into the decorated class (Section 4, p. 11).

Because «mixin» is non-sortal, it does not supply a uniform principle of identity. The profile constraints state that non-sortals, including «mixin», cannot specialize sortals, and that semi-rigid classifiers such as «mixin» cannot specialize anti-rigid classifiers (Table 1, p. 13). The source also states that the proposed profile does not explicitly capture the ontological nature of instances of non-sortal classes (Section 4, p. 12).
