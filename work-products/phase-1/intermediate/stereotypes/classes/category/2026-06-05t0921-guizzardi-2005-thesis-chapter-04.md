# Category

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

A `category` represents a rigid mixin. In the source, this means a non-sortal classifier that subsumes different kinds while representing a property treated as essential to all its instances.

As a mixin, `category` is a dispersive universal and therefore does not supply a principle of identity for its instances. The principle of identity must instead be supplied by the relevant substance sortal instantiated by each individual.

The proposed profile constrains `category` as a non-sortal. It must be defined as an abstract class, and its supertype cannot be a `kind`, `subkind`, `phase`, `role`, or `roleMixin`. The source also states that rigid mixins, including categories, cannot be subsumed by anti-rigid classifiers such as role mixins.
