# Category

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to the supplied source, `«category»` is the OntoUML 2 stereotype for rigid non-sortals (Section 4.1, p. 9). The source characterizes a category as a dispersive type that aggregates essential properties common to different rigid sortals (Section 2, p. 4). As a non-sortal, a category does not provide a uniform principle of identity and its instances may follow different identity principles. In OntoUML 2, category instances are also not constrained to a single ontological nature, allowing the category to range across endurant types when the taxonomy supports that generalization (Section 4.1, pp. 9-10).
