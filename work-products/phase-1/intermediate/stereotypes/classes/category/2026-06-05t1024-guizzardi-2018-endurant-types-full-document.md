# Category

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `category` as a rigid, relationally independent non-sortal: it aggregates essential properties common to different rigid sortals without supplying a uniform principle of identity (Section 2, p. 5). In the formal theory, `category` is defined as the combination of rigidity and non-sortality (Section 3, pp. 7-8). The proposed profile gives «category» a direct UFO counterpart and reflects these properties into the decorated class (Section 4, p. 11).

As a non-sortal stereotype, «category» is not used to provide or carry a single principle of identity. The profile constraints state that non-sortals, including «category», cannot specialize sortals, and that rigid types such as «category» cannot specialize anti-rigid types (Table 1, p. 13). The source also states that the proposed profile does not explicitly capture the ontological nature of instances of non-sortal classes (Section 4, p. 12).
