# Datatype

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 grounds datatypes in quality structures. A quality dimension associated with a quality universal may be represented as a datatype in a conceptual-model ontology. Source locators: pp. 246-247; p. 251.
- The source distinguishes objects from values: datatype instances are values without identity, whereas quality individuals are bona fide individuals that inhere in bearers. This distinction supports using datatypes for qualia or value regions rather than for the quality individuals themselves. Source locators: pp. 246-249.
- For complex quality domains, the source supports structured datatypes: constituent quality dimensions may be represented as fields, and constraints among the dimensions may be represented as constraints over those fields. Source locator: p. 251.
- Datatype constraints should reflect the geometry, topology, or ordering structure of the underlying quality structure. This includes constraints internal to a dimension and constraints relating multiple integral dimensions in a domain. Source locators: p. 251; p. 276.
