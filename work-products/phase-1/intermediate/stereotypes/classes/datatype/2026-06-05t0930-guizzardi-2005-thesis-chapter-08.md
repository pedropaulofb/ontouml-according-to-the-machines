# Datatype

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Section 8.2.2 gives an ontological interpretation of UML DataType as a quality structure. A datatype associated with an attribute represents the codomain of the attribute function represented by that attribute.

The source distinguishes datatypes from multiply instantiated universals. A datatype is treated as an abstract individual set whose members are pure values that can be multiply referred to, rather than as a universal with ordinary instances. For structured datatypes, the source maps multidimensional quality structures, or quality domains, to structured datatypes; their fields represent integral quality dimensions, and their constructors should ensure that values are provided for all integral dimensions.
