# Collective

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source analyzes one possible interpretation of powertype instances as collectives or mereological sums of instances of an associated type. On this account, the collective corresponds to the population or extension of a lower-level type, with membership determined by instantiation of that type.

The source treats this interpretation as initially attractive for resultant properties, because some properties attributed to powertype instances can be derived from the properties of their current members. However, the source rejects the collective-as-powertype-instance interpretation as insufficient. A mereological sum has an extensional identity criterion, so membership changes would produce a different individual. It also depends on the existence of members, which conflicts with the requirement that a powertype instance may persist without current living members or current manifestation.

Under the preferred account, collectives are better understood as rigid embodiments or populations that may manifest a variable embodiment at a world or time. The collective is therefore relevant as a manifestation-level entity, not as the stable identity of the powertype instance itself.
