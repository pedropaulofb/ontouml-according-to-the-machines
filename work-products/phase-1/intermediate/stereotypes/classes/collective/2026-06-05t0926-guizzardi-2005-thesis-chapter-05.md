# Collective

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source grounds `Collective` in the treatment of collections as conceptual wholes. Collections differ from quantities because they are not homeomerous aggregates: their parts need not be of the same kind as the whole, and they are not modeled through infinite divisibility.

The source supports treating a collective as a cognitively meaningful integral whole rather than as an arbitrary mereological sum. A collective is unified by a relation that determines a maximal collection of members for a modeling context. Within this account, members are related to the collective through a uniform member role type, making collectives a limiting case of functional complexes with a uniform internal structure.

The source explicitly supports `«collective»` for the kind whose instances are collections. It also allows non-extensional identity for collectives: depending on the conceptualization, a collective need not be identical merely to the sum of its current members.
