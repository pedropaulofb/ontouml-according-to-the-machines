# Kind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source characterizes Kind as a rigid sortal universal that supplies a uniform principle of identity to its instances. Kind is distinguished from Subkind, Phase, and Role because those sortals carry an inherited identity principle rather than supplying one (Section 2.1, pp. 3-4).

As a specialization constraint, a Kind cannot specialize another sortal universal, because doing so would make it inherit a principle of identity while also supplying one. A rigid universal may not specialize an anti-rigid universal, so Kind specializations are also excluded from anti-rigid supertypes (Section 2.2, p. 4).

For second-order modeling, a domain second-order type specializing Kind must have a rigid mixin as base type, identified in the source as an instance of Category. Such a second-order type partitions the category by the principle of identity supplied by its instances (Section 5.1.1, p. 8).
