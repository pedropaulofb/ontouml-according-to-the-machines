# Kind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source characterizes OntoUML kinds as rigid types that capture essential properties of their instances and provide a uniform principle of identity for those instances. This characterization is used to contrast OntoUML with languages that do not directly represent modal notions such as rigidity, anti-rigidity, and identity criteria.

The source also uses the kind/substance-sortal account in its analysis of powertypes. Under the preferred variable-embodiment interpretation, a powertype can be modeled as a substantial universal whose instances are endurants. The kind-like contribution is the provision of a stable principle of identity that determines which changes an instance can undergo while remaining numerically the same.

For Phase 1 purposes, the source supports describing kind as the rigid sortal basis for identity and essential classification. It also supports the claim that this identity-based account enables the later use of OntoUML design patterns for classifying the same individual through both rigid and anti-rigid types.
