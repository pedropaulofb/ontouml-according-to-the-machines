# Kind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

Chapter 7 uses Kind as a rigid substance sortal that supplies a principle of identity. In the role-modeling pattern of section 7.1, kinds supply the identity principles carried by sortal role subclasses.

The source uses this commitment to address role classifiers whose instances come from different kinds. Such a classifier is non-sortal and cannot itself supply identity; each instance must be covered by a sortal role subtype that carries the identity principle supplied by an appropriate kind (section 7.1).

For modal parthood, Chapter 7 contrasts rigid universals such as kinds with anti-rigid universals such as roles. When the whole universal is rigid, the guard predicate used in the generalized de dicto formulation is modally constant; in that case, the generalized formulation is equivalent to the de re formulation and the parts can be treated as truly essential (section 7.2).

This contribution is limited to what Chapter 7 says about Kind in relation to role modeling and modal parthood; it is not a complete Kind profile.
