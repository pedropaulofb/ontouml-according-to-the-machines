# Kind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `kind` as a rigid sortal endurant type that provides a uniform principle of identity for its instances (Section 2, p. 4). In the proposed OntoUML 2.0 profile, «kind» is retained for ultimate sortals whose instances are substantials (Section 4, p. 10). A class decorated as «kind» therefore combines two commitments in this source: it identifies the ontological nature of its instances as substantial and supplies their principle of identity.

The formal theory supports the constraints that kinds are rigid, mutually disjoint, cannot specialize a different kind, and anchor sortal hierarchies as unique ultimate sortals (Section 3, pp. 7-9). The profile-level constraints further state that a class representing a kind cannot specialize another kind and that a class cannot specialize more than one kind (Table 1, p. 13).
