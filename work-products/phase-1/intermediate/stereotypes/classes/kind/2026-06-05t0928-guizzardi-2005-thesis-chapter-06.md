# Kind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 uses the notion of kind in connection with sortal universals: universals that provide a principle of identity for the objects they classify. The source contrasts these with characterizing universals, which do not provide such a principle. Source locator: p. 202.
- In its account of substantials, the source states that objects have principles of identity supplied by the kinds, or substance sortals, that they instantiate. This distinguishes objects from amounts of matter, whose identity is determined mereologically. Source locator: p. 216.
- For conceptual modeling, the source's supported contribution is that identity-supplying universals may be represented as concrete classes, whereas non-sortal substantial universals must not be given direct instances. Source locator: p. 244.
