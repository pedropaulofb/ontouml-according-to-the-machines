# Kind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to the supplied source, `«kind»` is the OntoUML 2 stereotype for an ultimate sortal whose instances are regular objects, i.e., substantials. In the revised profile, `«kind»` is one of the ultimate-sortal stereotypes used to identify both the ontological nature of an endurant type's instances and the identity principle that applies to them (Section 4.1, p. 8). The source grounds this in the formal theory by treating every endurant as instantiating a unique ultimate sortal and by requiring non-kind sortals to specialize a unique ultimate sortal (Section 3; Section 4.1, pp. 7-9). Within OntoUML 2, `«kind»` is therefore preserved for object kinds, while relators, modes, and qualities receive their own ultimate-sortal stereotypes.
