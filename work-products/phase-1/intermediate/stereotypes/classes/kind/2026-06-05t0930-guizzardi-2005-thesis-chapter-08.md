# Kind

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.1.2 and table 8-1, `kind` is introduced as a concrete specialization of the abstract Substance Sortal metaclass in the ontologically well-founded UML profile. In this source, a kind is a rigid, relationally independent object universal that supplies a principle of identity for its instances. Chapter 8 uses `kind` in the narrower sense of a functional-complex kind, after distinguishing quantity kinds, collective kinds, and functional-complex kinds.

The source situates `kind` within the broader mapping from UML Class to monadic universal and, more specifically, from class-diagram classes representing substantial universals to object universals. As an ultimate substance sortal, `kind` participates in the source's general substance-sortal constraints: every concrete substantial object represented in a model must instantiate, directly or indirectly, a substance sortal; a substantial object cannot instantiate more than one ultimate substance sortal; and a substance sortal cannot be specialized from anti-rigid universals such as phase, role, or roleMixin.
