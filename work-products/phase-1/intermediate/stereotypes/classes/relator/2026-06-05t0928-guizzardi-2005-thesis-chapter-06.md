# Relator

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 treats relators as relational moments: moment individuals that depend on a plurality of individuals and mediate material relations. Source locators: p. 213; pp. 236-237.
- A relator is composed of qua individuals sharing the same foundation. The source requires a relator to mediate at least two distinct individuals, and it stipulates that the bearer of a relator is the mereological sum of the individuals it mediates. Source locators: pp. 239-241.
- A relator universal is a universal whose instances are relators. The source uses relator universals as the basis for defining material relations whose instances are tuples of mediated entities. Source locators: pp. 241-242.
- For conceptual modeling, the source favors explicit representation of relator universals as stereotyped classes, separated from the derived material relation and connected to mediated entity classes through mediation relations. This avoids construct overload and reduces cardinality ambiguity. Source locators: pp. 259-262.
