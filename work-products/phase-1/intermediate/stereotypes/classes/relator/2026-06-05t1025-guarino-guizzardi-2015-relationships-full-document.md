# Relator

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source treats relators as the OntoUML construct for reified relationships that make extrinsic or material relations hold. A relator is not analyzed as a tuple, a fact, or merely an event-like entity; it is characterized as an endurant that can mediate other endurants and thereby account for the holding of the corresponding relation.

In this account, a relator is a synchronous truthmaker for a relation: its existence at a time is what supports the relation holding at that time. The relator also accounts for how the relation develops over time, because it can bear and undergo qualitative change while preserving its identity.

The source refines the earlier trope-based account of relators by treating a relationship as a variable embodiment constituted, at each time it exists, by a mereological sum of relational qualities. These qualities include a nuclear part, whose existence entails that the relation holds, and a dependent shell, whose qualities describe contingent aspects of the relationship's development.

As an endurant, each relator instantiates a kind that supplies identity conditions through time and constrains admissible changes. The source also restricts relators to genuine relationships: there is no relator when a historical or relational connection leaves no relevant qualitative impact on any relatum.
