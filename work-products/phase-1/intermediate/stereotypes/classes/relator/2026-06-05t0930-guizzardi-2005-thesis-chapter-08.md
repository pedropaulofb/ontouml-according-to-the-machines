# Relator

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to section 8.2.2 and table 8-2, `relator` represents a relator universal, i.e., a relational moment universal. Every instance of a relator universal is existentially dependent on at least two distinct entities.

The source introduces `relator` because the original UML metamodel lacks constructs for relator universals. In the proposed profile, every `relator` must be directly or indirectly connected to an association end of at least one `mediation` relation. The source also constrains a relator by requiring the sum of the minimum cardinalities on the mediated ends to be at least two. Relators are treated as the ontological foundation for material relations, and their representation has priority over the optional representation of derived material relations.
