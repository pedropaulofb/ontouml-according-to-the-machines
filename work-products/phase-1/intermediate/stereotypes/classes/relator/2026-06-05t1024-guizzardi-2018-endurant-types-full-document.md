# Relator

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `relator` as a subtype of moment: relators are existentially dependent individuals that connect entities and depend on at least two distinct entities (Section 2, pp. 3-4). The formal theory places relators among the leaf categories of endurants and defines relator types as endurant types whose instances are relators (Section 3, p. 8).

For OntoUML profile interpretation, the source is transitional. It states that in the current version of OntoUML, «relator» carried information about the nature of instances of a moment type, because the language was silent about modal properties of moment types (Section 4, p. 10). In the proposed OntoUML 2.0 profile, «relator» is discontinued and replaced at the ultimate-sortal level by a distinct stereotype for relator kinds (Section 4, pp. 10-11). Therefore, this source contributes to a `relator` page by clarifying the ontological nature of relators and by recording that the proposed profile separates instance nature from modal properties for relator types.
