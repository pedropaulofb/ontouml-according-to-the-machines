# Phase

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

In this source, a phase is an anti-rigid sortal endurant type. A phase classifies its instances contingently, but its classification condition is intrinsic. The source contrasts phases with roles, since roles are also anti-rigid sortals but depend on relational classification conditions.

Because phases are sortals, the source's sortality account implies that a phase specializes a kind and carries the kind-based identity principle of its instances.
