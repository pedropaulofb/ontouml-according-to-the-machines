# Phase

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source contributes a phase-related interpretation for modeling terminated endurants under historical semantics. Because past events and their dependent endurants accumulate in the universe of discourse, termination is not treated as removal from the universe of discourse. Instead, termination is treated as a change in phase.

In the proposed event extension, `<<termination>>` relates an event type to a class stereotyped `<<phase>>`. The phase is instantiated by an endurant when it takes on a historical nature after termination. In that phase, the source states that the endurant has immutable properties analogous to those of past events.

This contribution is limited to the use of `<<phase>>` in the context of termination and historical semantics. The source does not provide a full general profile for the `<<phase>>` stereotype.
