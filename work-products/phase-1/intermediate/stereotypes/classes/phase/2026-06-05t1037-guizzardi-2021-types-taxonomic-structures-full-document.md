# Phase

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

According to the supplied source, `«phase»` is the OntoUML 2 stereotype for externally independent anti-rigid sortals whose instances share an ontological nature and follow a unique identity principle (Section 4.1, p. 9). The paper characterizes phases as relationally independent types defined by contingent intrinsic instantiation conditions (Section 2, p. 4). As anti-rigid sortals, phases classify instances contingently without replacing the identity principle supplied by their ultimate sortal. In OntoUML 2, phases are generalized beyond substantial types and can apply to other endurant-type taxonomies when supported by contingent intrinsic conditions (Introduction; Section 4.1, pp. 2, 9).
