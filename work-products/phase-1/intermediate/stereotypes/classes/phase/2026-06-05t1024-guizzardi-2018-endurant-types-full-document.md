# Phase

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

This source treats `phase` as an anti-rigid sortal whose instantiation conditions are contingent, intrinsic, and relationally independent (Section 2, p. 4). Phases contrast with roles because roles depend on relational conditions, while phases are independent in the proposed profile's distinction between the two anti-rigid sortal stereotypes (Section 2, pp. 4-5; Section 4, p. 11).

The paper's revision extends phases beyond substantial types to endurant types in general, including moment types (Introduction, pp. 2-3; Section 4, p. 11). In the proposed profile, «phase» specializes an ultimate kind stereotype from the proposed profile and remains subject to the general constraints on anti-rigid sortals: a class cannot specialize more than one kind, and rigid or semi-rigid classifiers cannot specialize anti-rigid classifiers such as «phase» (Table 1, p. 13).
