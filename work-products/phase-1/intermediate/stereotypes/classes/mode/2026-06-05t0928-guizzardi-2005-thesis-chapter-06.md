# Mode

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

- Chapter 6 defines a mode as an intrinsic moment individual that is not a quality. Modes differ from qualities because they are not directly associated with a single quality structure, even though they may bear other moments. Source locators: pp. 237-238.
- Modes are moments and therefore necessarily inhere in a bearer. The source also identifies externally dependent modes as modes whose existence depends on an individual independent of their bearer. Source locator: p. 238.
- The source supports explicit representation of mode universals in conceptual models when the modeled moment needs identity, properties of its own, or relations to other entities. In such cases, the mode universal should be represented explicitly and related to the characterized universal by characterization. Source locators: pp. 251-253.
- The source compares mode universals to weak entity types because their instances depend on uniquely determined bearers and are removed when their bearers cease to exist. This comparison is grounded in the inherence and non-transferability constraints on moments. Source locators: pp. 252-253.
