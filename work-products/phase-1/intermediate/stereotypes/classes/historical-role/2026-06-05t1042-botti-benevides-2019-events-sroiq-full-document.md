# Historical Role

## Source-Specific Description Contribution

The source uses the term `ProcessualRole`; it does not use the later target label in the cited passages. The source-specific contribution for this page is therefore provisional and should be reviewed before consolidation under a final stereotype label.

In UFO-B, `ProcessualRole` is an `ObjectUniversal` played by objects and induced by participation universals (p. 5, Figure 1; p. 15, axiom a8; p. 17, axioms a99, a113, a115). The source characterizes processual roles as classifiers of endurants according to how those endurants participate in events (p. 10, Section 3.7). It explicitly frames this as a synchronic classification inferred from a diachronic, historical classification (p. 10, Section 3.7).

The `plays` relation is an instantiation relation from `Object` to `ProcessualRole` (p. 10-11, Section 3.7). A processual role is induced by a unique `ParticipationUniversal`, and participation universals classify events (p. 10-11, Section 3.7). The source gives both sufficient and necessary FOL conditions for role playing: object participation in an atomic event, or in a participation having that atomic event as part, grounds the object's playing of the processual role induced by the corresponding participation universal (p. 11, Section 3.7, R1-R2).
